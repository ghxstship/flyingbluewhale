## Tallies

- **ATLVS: 7 Differentiated · 4 Present · 9 Partial · 4 Absent** (a11y row unjudged for competitors).
- The differentiation is concentrated exactly where the brief predicted: experiential-production workflows (portals, credentialing, advancing, field workforce, offline, notifications) — and **every Differentiated row carries an internal-audit caveat** that undermines it in practice (NV-1's unreachable flagship being the sharpest example).
- The Absent set (entity search, AI-on-records, undo, custom fields) is shared table-stakes across all three competitors — these are the parity exposures.
- The Partial set is mostly "built but not wired" (bulk ops, palette, field errors, views) — unusually cheap parity wins, because the engineering is done.

## Reading Guide for Phase 4

Three strategic implications drive the recommendations ranking:

1. **Close the wired-but-dead gaps first** — bulk ops, palette flattening, field errors, and the orphaned advancing surface are S-effort items that convert existing engineering into visible capability.
2. **Defend the moat before extending it** — the offline lead is shrinking (Odoo 19.3) and the credentialing/portal/mobile differentiators all carry Critical/High UX defects. Fixing SC-1/SC-4/PF-1/PF-3 protects claims the marketing site already makes.
3. **AI-on-records is the one Absent that compounds** — all three competitors made agentic AI their 2026 headline; ATLVS's chat-only AI will read as a generation behind by year-end. The domain schema (lifecycles, assignments, scan events) is unusually well-suited to permission-aware AI actions.
