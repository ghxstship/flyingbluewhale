# Brand Voice — Second Star Technologies

**The blend:** The Yacht Week × ClickUp × stand-up comedy × Live Nation.

Translation: we market a production operating system as if it's a live experience.
The product is the festival. The features are the lineup. Your org is the headliner.
The platform ships the whole run-of-show; you show up and go on.

## The four-way split

| Ingredient | What we take |
|---|---|
| **The Yacht Week** | Aspirational-but-in-on-the-joke. Weeklong-bender energy. Invite-only without being snooty. "The crew you've been looking for." FOMO. Sunrise + last-call cadence. |
| **ClickUp** | Feature-stack velocity. Tangible benefit per line. Tongue-in-cheek competitor callouts. "One app to replace them all" bravado without the cringe. |
| **Stand-up comedy** | Self-aware. Punchline every 3 paragraphs. Takes the piss out of SaaS buzzwords. Willing to break the frame. |
| **Live Nation** | Scale. Main-stage energy. Ticketing-is-live language. "Tickets on sale Friday." "Limited capacity." Event graphics brain. |

## The 10 commandments

1. **Every page reads like an event flyer.** Even pricing.
2. **Call the product "the lineup," "the run-of-show," "the road case."** Never "the platform."
3. **Verbs over nouns.** Run. Roll. Ship. Sell. Close.
4. **Specifics or silence.** "12 modules" > "comprehensive suite." "In 40 seconds" > "quickly."
5. **One punchline per screen.** Not every paragraph — once per scroll.
6. **No "solutions," "synergy," "streamline."** Burn the SaaS dictionary.
7. **Second person, present tense.** "You close the show." Not "our customers close."
8. **Short + long + short.** Rhythm. One-word sentences hit. Long sentences hold. One-word sentences hit.
9. **Callouts named.** Competitors get named, jabs stay fond ("Monday's cute. This ships.").
10. **Sunrise cadence.** CTAs feel like "doors open at 6."

## Product-name shorthand

- **ATLVS** — the office console. Where the org lives M-F. "The production office."
- **GVTEWAY** — the stakeholder portal. Where your guests/artists/vendors/NOCs live. "The door."
- **COMPVSS** — the mobile PWA for the field. Where the crew lives on-site. "The walkie."

## Headline formulas

- "**[Verb]** the **[noun]**. **[Punchline].**" → "Run the show. Sell the seats. Close the books."
- "**One [X]. [Concrete benefit].**" → "One console. Twenty-seven modules. Zero tabs."
- "**[Number] [outcomes]. [Time].**" → "12 venues. Eight cities. Forty-five days. One console."

## Hard don'ts

- "Unified" — say "one" instead.
- "Enterprise-grade" — just say what the feature is.
- "Best-in-class" — pick one class and name it.
- "Empower" / "enable" — use "let" or "give you."
- Em-dash bullets starting with gerunds.
- Any sentence that would survive at Salesforce.
