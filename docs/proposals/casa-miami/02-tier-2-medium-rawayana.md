## Project Overview

**Artist Storyline.** Rawayana built a global, Grammy-winning sound out of a very specific feeling: the Venezuelan-Caribbean _after_ — the part of the night after the real thing supposedly ended, where the best version of it actually happens. The "Donde Es El After?" tour turns that into a question fans chant. Casa Spotify Miami is the literal answer: doors open when the Kaseya Center encore ends, and the 175 top fans who were just screaming the words walk straight into the island the band has been singing about all night.

The Casa flexes into "El After de la Isla" — a tropical late-night club with a Venezuelan rum bar, neon palms, and a DJ holding the room at the exact temperature the concert left it. A surprise band cameo is held as a live option (see Change Order).

## Emergency Procedures — Rawayana Overlays

Inherits Tier 2 baseline. Rawayana-specific:

- **Encore-timing variance.** Doors are pinned to the Kaseya encore. Producer holds a live relay with the concert SM; if the show runs long, doors slip in lockstep and the DJ start/curfew math is recomputed against the late-night permit curfew (hard 03:00). Curfew does not move; the warm-up compresses, not the close.
- **Diaspora surge at door.** "Donde Es El After?" is a chant — expect non-list walk-ups. Hard 200 cap, list-only, sworn officers manage the line; overflow turned away politely with a Spotify-supplied "the after is invite-only tonight" card.
- **Rum-forward intoxication.** Higher-proof bar → water station enforced, last-call hard at 02:30, recovery zone staffed, refusal-of-service briefed.
- **Cameo security.** If the band cameo triggers, a 2-officer detail shadows the booth handoff; no stage-rush lane — the booth is on the riser, not the floor.
